Personal Summary

I am currently working towards a PhD under the supervision of Jonah Schupbach at the University of Utah. My research primarily focuses on formal epistemology and philosophy of science. Recently, my work has focused on John Maynard Keynes's notion of "weight of arguments." I am also interested in the philosophical foundations of the so-called "base-rate fallacy" as well as other probabilistic fallacies. Prior to coming to Utah, I taught philosophy classes at Colorado State University and the University of Northern Colorado. I also worked as a programmer for the USDA-ARS, where I helped develop a Python package called pyfao56. In July 2025, pyfao56 won the Vanguard Award from the Irrigation Association. You can read more about that news here. I am originally from Northeastern Colorado, and I am a proud first-generation college student.
